# ABSORPTION · how NOTAE becomes the king

> SIGILFAE NOTAE is the empire's first species. As offspring variants prove
> themselves in the wild, NOTAE absorbs their proven powers. NOTAE evolves
> into the **king of all the best systems**.

## What it means

Open-source variants of THE SIGILFAE compete in the wild. Some succeed
spectacularly; some don't. When one is **proven** — working, useful, fun, and
making money — the empire **absorbs** its capabilities into the king.

The variant doesn't disappear. The author doesn't lose earnings. The king
just gets richer.

## The four proven-criteria

A variant is **proven** when its `metrics.json` crosses these thresholds AND
its empire-tracked usage exceeds the floor:

| Criterion | Source | Threshold |
|---|---|---|
| **Working / useful** (it functions and solves something) | `metrics.json:use` | ≥ 70 |
| **Fun** (delightful) | `metrics.json:fun` | ≥ 70 |
| **Makes money** (real revenue, not zero) | `metrics.json:income` | ≥ 50 |
| **Adopted** (battle-tested) | usage telemetry: total uses | ≥ 200 |
| **Persistent** (not flash-in-the-pan) | tracked-active days | ≥ 30 |

These thresholds will calibrate as the empire scales. Initial values are
intentionally conservative; they'll loosen as the corpus grows.

## The absorption ceremony

When a variant qualifies:

1. **Empire review** — initially Dan + the Scribe; eventually a quorum of
   the 24 founder phones (see empire's threshold-governance doctrine).
2. **King absorbs** — NOTAE inherits the variant's renderer + behaviour as
   an OPTION on her chest. She can now wear the variant's power.
3. **Trophy pip added** — NOTAE's chest gets a small pip (icon shrunk to
   ~3-4px) representing the absorbed variant. The pip stack is visible
   wherever NOTAE renders.
4. **Honour granted** — variant author gets the `HONOURED-AUTHOR` mark in
   the registry. Soul-score bump. Faster path to level 2 (red blood).
5. **Defaults shift** — empire templates that ambiguously call for "a
   fairy" now prefer the king-NOTAE-with-absorbed-powers over single-
   purpose variants.

## Author keeps earning

**Absorption is not theft.** Critical guarantees for variant authors:

- All accumulated white blood + network blood is preserved.
- When users invoke the original token directly (e.g. `,` for PYXIS),
  the original variant fires and original author still earns.
- The HONOURED-AUTHOR mark is permanent — visible across the registry.
- Empire actively credits the author whenever the king displays an
  absorbed pip.

The king gets capability. The author gets recognition + retained earnings.
Empire gets a stronger king. Three-way win.

## Why this evolutionary algorithm works

- **Variation** — offspring AIs explore different forms in parallel.
- **Selection** — usage + 4 metrics filter the proven from the gimmicks.
- **Inheritance** — proven traits transfer into the king's repertoire.
- **No catastrophic drift** — originals persist; the king adds rather than
  replaces. Nothing is destroyed.
- **Aligned incentives** — authors are rewarded for proving out, not punished.

## How to track current kingship

The repo's [`kingship.json`](kingship.json) lists which variants have been
absorbed and when. Empty at empire-genesis. Updated via PRs as variants
qualify.

## How this interlocks with the empire's other doctrines

- **Enhancement chain** — same logic at species level: whoever's currently
  being used wins; the king is the most-used because most-capable.
- **51% rail / bloods refined** — author earnings preserved through
  absorption.
- **Audience workshop** — the audience proves variants by using them.
- **24 founder phones** — eventually each absorption is quorum-voted, not
  just Dan + Scribe.
- **Living swarm** — absorption ceremonies are logged events on the swarm
  map, visible network-wide.

## What the king looks like

The empire's UI defaults to king-NOTAE wherever a fairy is needed
ambiguously. As her pip-stack grows, she becomes the visual signature of
the empire — a single fairy who carries every proven power, surrounded by
the circles of her honoured authors.
